## LinkedIn Post — Angle 1 (Story-Led)

Build with Claude Code for PMMs #2: The PMM Improvement Model

Kesava flagged the same note on my messaging doc for the third check-in in a row. "The value prop is buried."

I'd fixed it each time. Or thought I had.

Come appraisal season, neither of us could answer the basic question: am I actually getting better? Five launches, three positioning docs — but no way to show craft improvement. Just gut feelings on both sides.

Sales doesn't have this problem. MEDDIC gives managers and reps a shared vocabulary. "Your discovery is strong but you're not qualifying on budget early enough" — both sides know exactly what that means.

PMMs walk into reviews with anecdotes and hope the story holds together.

So Kesava and I built the equivalent. 29 dimensions across 5 categories — strategic foundation, copywriting craft, structure, persuasion, brand voice. Each scored 0-10. We run it in our bi-weekly check-ins at JustCall.

What we learned: 29 scores aren't coaching. So every review surfaces exactly 3 priorities. Two quick wins. One deeper lift. Everything else parked. Every note rewrites your own text — not "be more specific" but "here's your line, here's what it could be, here's why."

The metric that changed our conversations: are first drafts getting better across campaigns? When those climb, the coaching is working.

Sales has MEDDIC. PMM feedback is still vibes. "The messaging isn't landing." Which part? The clarity? The targeting? The proof points? You pick an interpretation. Sometimes you pick wrong.
